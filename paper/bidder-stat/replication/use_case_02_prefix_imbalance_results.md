## Reading

- **Endpoint exactness alone is not enough.** Lexicographic order has the same terminal counts as any permutation of the block, but its worst prefix imbalance is `94.863` at the first digit boundary.
- **BIDDER removes the enumeration pathology.** The fixed paper key gives `Dmax = 7.520`, and all 64 checked BIDDER keys remain below 20% of the lexicographic value.
- **BIDDER is not being claimed as a uniformly random permutation.** Across the checked keys its prefix imbalance is measurably larger than the random-permutation baseline. The claim is narrower: keyed order breaks the lexicographic leading-digit run while retaining exact endpoint composition and reproducibility.
