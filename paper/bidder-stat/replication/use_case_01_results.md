# §7.1 — Stratified survey design with exact leading-digit strata

Generated by `replication/use_case_01_stratified_survey.py`. Substrate-leveraged use case (§3.2 clauses 1–2 do the work; the cipher delivers per-stratum keyed random access).

`(b, d) = (10, 4)`; stratum size `b^(d-1) = 1,000`. Proportional design weights `α_j ∈ [0.1, 0.5, 1.0]` per stratum; total sample size `N_total = α · (b−1) · b^(d-1)`. BIDDER row: per-stratum count is exactly `⌊α · b^(d-1)⌋`. i.i.d. row: `1,000` trials of post-stratification on uniform i.i.d. samples from the block.

## Reading

- **BIDDER row.** Per-stratum count is `⌊α · b^(d-1)⌋` exactly across all 9 strata; max deviation = 0. The substrate guarantee (§3.2 clause 1) is structural — exact at any α; cipher delivers a keyed prefix of the per-stratum permutation, streaming and reproducible.
- **i.i.d.-then-post-stratify row.** Mean per-stratum count tracks `N_total / (b−1)` as expected; max deviation across 9 strata grows as `O(sqrt(N_total · (b−2)/(b−1)²))`. At `α = 1.0` (N_total = 9,000), the 99th-percentile max deviation across 1000 trials is the order of one 99th-percentile binomial deviate from the mean of 9 independent strata.
- **Headline payoff.** Exact per-stratum counts let stratified-sample variance estimators apply design weights `α_j` directly, without post-hoc adjustment for realised stratum sizes — the i.i.d.-then-post-stratify approach would otherwise carry a `O(sqrt(N_total))` correction.
- **Use something else when** the application tolerates `O(sqrt(N_total / k))` deviation in stratum sizes; standard proportional sampling with post-stratification is simpler.
