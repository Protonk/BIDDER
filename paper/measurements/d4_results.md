Per-backend numbers (C-direct cost is per-call constant-time within a backend; cycle-walking cost varies with `P` for Speck32 — see M1):

## Reading

- **Kernel cost is sub-µs.** Compared to M4's ~940 ns/elem for the cipher path through the Python wrapper, the C-direct number isolates the kernel from the ctypes round-trip. The ctypes overhead is the difference: M4_cipher_ns − bench_c_cipher_ns ≈ wrapper cost per call.
- **Backend matters.** The cycle-walking ratio bound (`MAX_CYCLE_WALK_RATIO = 64` → backend = `feistel` for `P < 2^26`) means the panel here uses Feistel for all small `P`. M1 covers the Speck32/64 regime at `P ≥ 2^26` and the cycle-walking-overhead curve.
- **§6.4 cites the C-direct row** as workload (3); §7.4's head-to-head comparison cites M3 as workload (2).
