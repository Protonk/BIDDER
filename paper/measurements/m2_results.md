## Reading

- **The realisation gap is significant and `(P, N)`-dependent.** At
  small `P` (200–500), ratios cluster within ~2× of the ideal. At
  larger `P` (5000–10000), ratios climb to 8–32×. At `P = 1000` the
  ratios are *below* 1 (0.1–0.2), indicating systematic
  bias-canceling on the chosen integrand at this period. The
  pattern reproduces stably at `n_keys = 2000` (Monte Carlo standard
  error on a variance estimator at this `n_keys` is ~3%, well below
  the observed deviations).

- **Backend.** All cells in this panel use the Feistel backend
  (Speck32 takes over at `P ≥ 2^26 ≈ 67M` per M1's measurement).
  The 8-round Feistel in `coupler.py` is intentionally lightweight
  (no library dependencies, ~50 lines) and is not a cryptographically
  strong PRP. The realisation gap is the empirical price for that
  choice.

- **What §4.5 cites from this table.** The realisation gap
  *exists*, *is measured*, and *is `(P, N)`-dependent*. Specific
  numbers worth reporting in §4.5 prose:
  - Best case: ratio ≈ 1 at small `P` (e.g., `(P, N) = (200, 20)` →
    1.022).
  - Worst case in the panel: ratio ≈ 32 at `(P, N) = (10000, 5000)`.
  - Anomalous regime: ratio < 1 at `P = 1000` (a function of how
    the Feistel's symmetries interact with `f(x) = sin(πx)` on a
    grid of size 1000).

- **Framing for §4.5.** This measurement is the only place in the
  paper where BIDDER's cipher quality is evaluated quantitatively.
  The substrate-and-cipher split (§4.1) puts the *exact* claims at
  the substrate (§3) and the *structural* claim at `N = P` (§4.4);
  the cipher's PRP quality is asked only to deliver "good enough"
  randomness for a permutation. The realisation gap is what
  "good enough" means in concrete terms here. Users requiring
  tight FPC realisation can drop in a stronger cipher (AES-based
  FF1, at substantially higher per-call cost — see §7.4's
  comparison).

- **§7.6 row reading.** The chosen `P = 2000` cell shows the gap
  peaking around `N = P/2` (ratio 6.8) and tapering at the
  extremes (ratio ~3 at `N = 0.1·P` and `N = 0.9·P`). The shape
  is reproducibly U-shaped in `N/P`; the magnitude of the peak
  scales with `P`.

- **What this finding does *not* invalidate.** §4.4's structural
  identity at `N = P` (variance machine-ε, key-independent) is
  unaffected; that's a multiset claim, not a permutation-quality
  claim. The §3 substrate's leading-digit uniformity is also
  unaffected (it's a counting claim about the digit-class block).
  The realisation gap is specific to §4.5 and lives there.
