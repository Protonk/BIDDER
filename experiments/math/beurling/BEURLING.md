# Beurling — PhD thesis brief

Two PhD theses, both from Vindas's group at Ghent, both squarely on
Beurling generalised primes:

1. **Debruyne, *Complex Tauberian theorems and applications to
   Beurling generalized primes*, Ghent 2017–18** (206 pp, supervisor
   Vindas). Two-part structure: Part I builds Tauberian theory
   (Ingham–Karamata, Wiener–Ikehara) abstractly; Part II applies it
   to Beurling primes (PNT equivalences, density theorems).

2. **Broucke, *Asymptotic methods in number theory and analysis*,
   Ghent 2021–22** (232 pp, supervisor Vindas, co-supervisor
   Debruyne). Self-identifies as a problem-solver's collection.
   Part I: a new random discretisation procedure + Malliavin's
   problems. Part II: unrelated analysis problems (Riemann's "other"
   function, fractional Zener wave equation — not relevant to
   BIDDER).

This brief consolidates the source material in
`sources/generalized/`: the two PhD theses above, the supplementary
slide decks (Broucke 2022, Vindas 2010), and Olofsson's 2010
paper. Bulk of the content is the theses; landscape framing
(directions of implication, well-behaved-system framework) and
the rigidity-at-`Re s = 0` section absorb the supplements.

**Notation.** Throughout, `log_k x` is the `k`-fold iterated
logarithm: `log_2 x = log log x`, `log_3 x = log log log x`, etc.
This follows Broucke's convention; the older Beurling literature
sometimes writes `log log x` directly.

## What the theses change about the working setup

### Generalised number systems "in the extended sense"

Both theses adopt a strictly broader definition than discrete primes:

> A *generalised number system in the extended sense* is a pair
> `(Pi, N)` of right-continuous, non-decreasing, unbounded
> functions on `[1, infinity)` with `Pi(1) = 0, N(1) = 1`, linked
> via
>
>     zeta(s) := integral_{1-}^infinity x^{-s} dN(x)
>             =  exp(integral_1^infinity x^{-s} dPi(x))
>
> on a half-plane of convergence.
>
> Equivalently: `dN = exp_*(dPi)` under multiplicative convolution.

(Debruyne §1.2.1; Broucke §1.1.) Discrete Beurling systems are then
the special case where `dPi` is supported on a discrete set with
unit masses at the primes plus `1/v` at `p^v`. The extended-sense
framework removes the discrete-primes and the unique-factorisation
scaffolding; it asks only for the convolution-exponential link
between two non-decreasing measures. **This is the framework in
which the Tauberian machinery lives.**

For BIDDER's `M_n`: there are two distinct candidate Pi-measures
that must be kept separate.

- **Candidate atom-power measure.** The naïve construction
  `dPi_{M_n}^{atom}(x) = sum_{atoms a, a^v <= x} (1/v) delta_{a^v}`
  is the Riemann prime-power measure of the **free** Beurling
  system generated by the atoms of `M_n`, ignoring relations.
  For UF systems this *is* the right `Pi`; for `M_n` it isn't,
  because `M_n` carries relations among ordered factorisations
  (e.g. `36 = 6 . 6 = 2 . 18` in `M_2`) that the free
  construction ignores.

- **Actual log measure of `zeta_{M_n}`.** This is the `dPi_{M_n}^{log}`
  defined by `log zeta_{M_n}(s) = integral x^{-s} dPi_{M_n}^{log}(x)`
  on the half-plane of convergence — i.e., the measure whose
  Mellin transform is `log zeta_{M_n}`. The repo already records
  this object: `Q_n(m) = [m^{-s}] log zeta_{M_n}(s)` is exactly
  its coefficient sequence (`algebra/Q-FORMULAS.md`), and the
  resulting measure is **signed**. Signed cancellation between
  ordered factorisations is the visible empirical content in
  `experiments/acm-flow/ACM-MANGOLDT.md`.

The Beurling extended-sense framework requires `dPi`
non-negative and non-decreasing. The atom-power candidate is
non-negative but matches the wrong system; the log-measure
candidate matches `zeta_{M_n}` but is signed. **These cannot be
reconciled.** The Mellin transform is injective on Stieltjes
measures, so a `dPi` compatible with `zeta_{M_n}` is determined
uniquely by `log zeta_{M_n} = integral x^{-s} dPi`. Since
`log zeta_{M_n}` has a signed Mellin coefficient sequence — the
sequence is `Q_n` (`algebra/Q-FORMULAS.md`), and explicit
negative mass is recorded in `experiments/acm-flow/ACM-MANGOLDT.md` —
**no non-negative `dPi` exists whose convolution-exponential is
`dN_{M_n}`**. The extended-sense Beurling framework therefore
does not accommodate `M_n` directly. Any Beurling-style theorem
must either be reformulated for signed log-measures, or applied
to a different (non-negative) template whose convolution-exponential
*approximates* `dN_{M_n}` without equalling it. The non-UF
algebra of `M_n` is the structural source of the obstruction;
the analytic obstruction is the signedness of `Q_n`.

## Landscape: directions of implication

Two pillars of Beurling theory deal with opposite directions of
implication. They are not the same problem.

**Direction A — `N` hypothesis ⇒ `pi` / `Pi` conclusion.**

| `N(x)` hypothesis | conclusion | source |
|---|---|---|
| `rho x + O(x / log^gamma x)`, `gamma > 3/2` | `pi(x) ~ x / log x` | Beurling 1937 |
| `rho x + O(x^theta)`, `theta < 1` | `pi(x) = Li(x) + O(x exp(-c sqrt(log x)))` | Landau 1903 |

**Direction B — `pi` / `Pi` hypothesis ⇒ `N` conclusion.**

| `pi` / `Pi` hypothesis | conclusion | source |
|---|---|---|
| `pi(x) = Li(x) + O(x / log^gamma x)`, `gamma > 1` | `N(x) ~ rho x` | Diamond 1977 |
| `Pi(x) = Li(x) + O(x^theta)`, `theta < 1` | `N(x) = rho x + O(x exp(-c' sqrt(log x log_2 x)))` | Hilberdink–Lapidus 2006 |
| same, optimal constant | `c' = sqrt(2(1 - theta))` | Broucke–Debruyne–Vindas 2020 |

Beurling 1937 (Direction A) and Hilberdink–Lapidus 2006 (Direction B)
are the prototypes for **Malliavin's two problems** — the deep
transfer-of-exponent questions in the centre of Broucke's thesis
(§3 below). Direction A says some `N`-hypothesis gives a
`pi`-conclusion; Direction B is the reverse. The Malliavin set-up
asks for the *sharp* exponent in each direction.

## Debruyne — the actionable part for BIDDER

### Tauberian theorems (Ch. 2), abstract

Debruyne's main Tauberian theorems are stated for arbitrary
Laplace transforms with local pseudofunction boundary behaviour;
they make **no reference to Beurling primes, no UF, no
multiplicative structure**. The cleanest representative:

> **Theorem 2.1.3.** Let `tau in L^1_loc(R)` be slowly decreasing,
> vanish on `(-infinity, 0)`, and have convergent Laplace
> transform `L{tau; s}` on `Re s > 0`. If
>
>     L{tau; s}  -  a/s^2  -  sum_n b_n / (s - i t_n)
>                            -  sum_m (c_m + d_m log^{k_m}(1/s)) / s^{beta_m + 1}
>
> has local pseudofunction boundary behaviour on `Re s = 0`, then
> `tau(x)` has the corresponding asymptotic expansion (sums of the
> singularity contributions plus `o(1)`).

Proof goes through a boundedness theorem (Theorem 2.2.1) that
allows one-sided (boundedly decreasing) Tauberian conditions, plus
a characterisation of local pseudofunctions (§2.3). Romanovski's
lemma is the key topological tool — it eliminates uniformity
requirements on exceptional null sets `E` of boundary
singularities. Earlier theorems (Korevaar; Arendt–Batty) needed
either no exceptional set or a uniformity condition; Debruyne
removes both.

**Portability statement.** The Beurling application (next
subsection) uses UF and the Euler product at two distinct steps:
*(i)* reading `-zeta'_P/zeta_P` as the Mellin transform of a
non-negative Chebyshev measure `psi_P`, which is automatic when
the log-measure is the (positive) von Mangoldt measure of a UF
system; *(ii)* deriving the boundary behaviour of that ratio
from the Euler-product log-derivative
`sum (log p_j) p_j^{-s} / (1 - p_j^{-s})`. The Tauberian theorem
runs only after both steps are in place.

For BIDDER's `M_n`, both steps need replacement. Step (i) is the
prerequisite flagged above: the log-measure of `zeta_{M_n}` is
signed, so `-zeta'_{M_n}/zeta_{M_n}` does **not** read as a
positive Chebyshev measure without further surgery. Step (ii)
likewise has no Euler product to differentiate. The abstract
Tauberian theorem is available as a black box; the derivation
that feeds it has to be redone end-to-end, and it is not obvious
that the input the theorem needs (a non-negative measure with
prescribed boundary behaviour) exists for `M_n`.

### PNT equivalences (Ch. 7), the explicit translation

> **Lemma 7.2.4 (Debruyne).** A generalised number system satisfies
> the PNT (`psi(x) ~ x`) if and only if
>
>     -zeta'(s)/zeta(s)  -  1/(s - 1)
>
> has local pseudofunction boundary behaviour on `Re s = 1`. The
> sharp Mertens relation `psi_1(x) := integral_1^x dpsi(t)/t = log x + c + o(1)`
> holds if and only if
>
>     -zeta'(s)/((s-1) zeta(s))  -  1/(s-1)^2  -  c/(s - 1)
>
> has local pseudofunction boundary behaviour on `Re s = 1`.

This is the cleanest two-way translation in the literature. Each
arithmetic asymptotic ⇔ a precise boundary-regularity property of
the zeta function.

Two main theorems then sharpen Diamond–Zhang's earlier
equivalences:

> **Theorem 7.1.1.** PNT + `N(x) = ax + o(x/log x)` +
> `integral |N(x) - ax|/x^2 dx < infinity` ⇒ sharp Mertens with
> constant `c = -1 - (1/a) integral_1^infinity (N(x) - ax)/x^2 dx`.
>
> **Theorem 7.1.4.** `M(x) = o(x)` and the same `L^1` condition
> imply `m(x) = sum mu(n_k)/n_k = o(1)`.

§7.4 constructs explicit examples showing these implications can
fail under weaker hypotheses (Proposition 7.1.3): there are
systems with PNT + the strongest `N(x) = ax + O(x/log x)` bound but
**without** sharp Mertens.

### Density of generalised integers (Ch. 8) — Diamond's L¹ criterion

> **Theorem 8.1.1 (Diamond 1977, reproved Tauberianly).** If
> `integral_2^infinity |Pi(x) - x/log x| dx/x^2 < infinity`, then
> there exists `a > 0` with `N(x) ~ a x`.

Diamond's original proof was elementary but intricate (subtle
decompositions of `dPi` plus an iterative procedure). Debruyne's
proof goes via local pseudofunction boundary behaviour of `zeta`
plus the distributional Wiener–Ikehara theorem. **The Tauberian
proof generalises** to weaker hypotheses than the L¹ condition
(see Debruyne Theorem 8.3.1).

## Broucke — what's new beyond Debruyne

### Random discretisation that beats DMVZ (Ch. 2)

Diamond–Montgomery–Vorhauer–Zhang (DMVZ, 2006) gave a
probabilistic construction: from a non-negative `f` with
`f(u) ≪ 1/log u` and divergent integral, you can find a discrete
prime sequence `(p_j)` with

    | sum_{p_j <= x} p_j^{-it} - integral_1^x u^{-it} f(u) du |
        ≪ sqrt(x) + sqrt(x log(|t| + 1) / log(x + 1)).

Broucke's main result improves the underlying `O(sqrt x)` to `O(1)`:

> **Theorem 2.1.2 (Broucke).** Let `F` be non-decreasing,
> right-continuous, with `F(1) = 0` and the Chebyshev bound
> `F(x) ≪ x/log x`. Then there exists a sequence of generalised
> primes `P = (p_j)_{j>=1}` with `|pi_P(x) - F(x)| <= 2` and the
> same exponential-sum bound as DMVZ. If `F` is continuous, the
> bound improves to `|pi_P(x) - F(x)| <= 1`.

The proof uses Hoeffding's inequality (instead of Kolmogorov's,
which DMVZ used) on independent random variables `cos(t log P_j)`
with `P_j` distributed on intervals `[q_{j-1}, q_j]` chosen to
have unit `dF`-mass.

**The well-behaved-system framework.** Hilberdink (2005) and
Hilberdink–Lapidus (2006) parametrise Beurling systems by the
exponents `(alpha, beta, gamma)`:

    Pi(x)  = Li(x) + O(x^{alpha + epsilon}),    every epsilon > 0,
    N(x)   = rho x + O(x^{beta + epsilon}),     every epsilon > 0,
    M(x)   = O(x^{gamma + epsilon}),            every epsilon > 0,

with no improvement at any `epsilon < 0`. Such a system is called
`[alpha, beta, gamma]`-well-behaved. Under RH the rational
integers are `[1/2, 0, 1/2]`. **Hilberdink (2005): every discrete
`[alpha, beta]`-system has `max(alpha, beta) >= 1/2`** — the
multiplicative-structure ceiling forbidding both prime and integer
counts from being simultaneously `O(x^{1/2 - epsilon})`. The
question of which `[alpha, beta]` are realisable below this
ceiling was open before the discretisation procedure of Theorem
2.1.2; partial answers (Zhang 2007: some `<= 1/2`-system;
Broucke–Vindas 2021: a `[0, 1/2]`-system) led up to the
`[0, 1/2, 1/2]` construction below.

Application — direct construction of a `[0, 1/2, 1/2]`-system:

> **Theorem 2.3.1.** There is a discrete Beurling generalised
> prime system `P` with
>
>     Pi_P(x) = Li(x) + O(log log x),
>     N_P(x) = x + O(x^{1/2} exp(c (log x)^{2/3})),
>     M_P(x) = O(x^{1/2} exp(c (log x)^{2/3})),
>
> and `Omega_pm` lower bounds matching `x^{1/2 - epsilon}`.

The novelty over earlier `[<= 1/2]`-results is that the `M_P`
Möbius bound is now also matched at `1/2`, completing the triple.

### Malliavin's problems solved (Ch. 3–5)

**Setup.** Define

    (P_alpha): pi(x) = Li(x) + O(x exp(-c log^alpha x))
    (N_beta) : N(x) = rho x + O(x exp(-c' log^beta x))

and

    alpha*(beta) := sup{alpha : (N_beta) ⇒ (P_alpha)}
                                                (Malliavin's 1st)
    beta*(alpha) := sup{beta : (P_alpha) ⇒ (N_beta)}
                                                (Malliavin's 2nd)

**State of the art at start of Broucke's thesis.**

- Diamond (1969): `(P_alpha)` ⇒ `(N_{alpha/(alpha+1)})`, so
  `beta*(alpha) >= alpha/(alpha + 1)`.
- Hall (1972): `(N_beta)` ⇒ `(P_{beta/7.91})` (later
  `beta/(beta + 6.91)`).
- DMVZ (2006), `theta in (1/2, 1)`: gave the *only* solved case,
  `alpha*(1) = 1/2`, by constructing a system with
  `N(x) = rho x + O(x^theta)` and `pi(x) = Li(x) + Omega_pm(x exp(-c sqrt(log x)))`.

**Broucke's two main contributions:**

> **Theorem 3.1.5.** For every `alpha in (0, 1]` and `c > 0`
> (with `c <= 1` if `alpha = 1`), there is a discrete Beurling
> system with `Pi - Li ≪ x exp(-c log^alpha x)` (or `≪ log_2 x` in
> the boundary case `alpha = c = 1`) **and**
>
>     N(x) - rho x = Omega_pm(x exp(-(c(alpha+1))^{1/(alpha+1)} (log x . log_2 x)^{alpha/(alpha+1)} (1 + b log_3 x / log_2 x))).

This shows Diamond's exponent `alpha / (alpha + 1)` is **sharp**.
Together with Diamond's theorem (which gives the matching upper
bound, with explicit constants — Theorem 3.1.4), Malliavin's
second problem is **completely solved**:

    beta*(alpha) = alpha / (alpha + 1).

The construction is delicate: a saddle-point analysis of a
specific zeta function (Bohr-style construction) producing
extremal oscillations for `N`.

> **Theorem 3.1.6.** For every `beta in (0, 1]`, there is a
> discrete Beurling system with `(N_beta)` and
>
>     pi(x) = Li(x) + O(x exp(-c log^{beta/(beta + 1)} x)),
>
> and matching `Omega_pm` lower bound.

Corollary 3.1.7: `alpha*(beta) <= beta/(beta + 1)`. The matching
lower bound (Hall: `alpha*(beta) >= beta/(beta + 6.91)`) is much
weaker. **Bateman–Diamond conjectured the upper bound is sharp**
(`alpha*(beta) = beta/(beta + 1)`); Malliavin's first problem
remains open in general. Broucke's open-problems appendix
sketches a Borel–Carathéodory + Jensen-formula approach that he
expects to eventually deliver this.

### Open problems Broucke flags (Appendix A)

Worth recording verbatim because they map the live research
front:

1. **Discretisation with two-sided control.** Theorem 2.1.2 gives
   `pi - F ≪ 1` and `N - rho x ≪ x^{1/2 + epsilon}`. Hilberdink's
   `max{alpha, beta} >= 1/2` says you can't simultaneously make
   both small. Can you get `O(x^beta)` on integers and
   `O(x^alpha)` on primes for `1/2 < alpha < 1` and `beta < 1/2`?
   (Open.)

2. **`[alpha, beta]`-system existence** for any
   `alpha, beta in [0, 1)` with `max(alpha, beta) >= 1/2`.
   Affirmative for `[0, 1/2]` (Theorem 2.3.1). General case open.

3. **Balazard's question for classical Dirichlet series** — does
   there exist a classical Dirichlet series `sum a_n n^{-s}` with
   exactly one zero in its half-plane of convergence? Broucke
   answers it positively for *general* Dirichlet series via
   Beurling-system construction (the Mobius series of the
   `[0, 1/2, 1/2]`-system). For classical Dirichlet series with
   integer support, open.

4. **DMVZ optimality for `theta <= 1/2`.** DMVZ optimality of
   Landau's PNT-with-`O(x^theta)`-bound is established for
   `theta > 1/2`; for `theta <= 1/2`, the template zeta function
   they use only continues to `Re s > 1/2`, and a different
   construction is needed. **Open.**

5. **Malliavin's first problem in general** — closing
   `alpha*(beta) = beta/(beta+1)` from below. Broucke expects this
   needs a Borel–Carathéodory + Jensen approach approximating an
   abstract `(Pi, N)` system by an explicitly-zeta-controlled
   sequence `(P_j)`.

## Olofsson — rigidity at `Re s = 0` (outside the theses)

Rikard Olofsson, *Properties of the Beurling generalized primes*
(2010), proves results not covered by the theses but on the same
machinery, applied at the line `Re s = 0` instead of `Re s = 1`.

> **Theorem 1.1 (Mertens for Beurling primes).** For any system
> with `N(x) = A x + o(x)`,
>
>     prod_{p <= x} (1 - 1/p)^{-1}  ~  A . e^gamma . log x.

For rational primes (`A = 1`) this gives the Meissel–Mertens
constant `M = 0.2614972...`. The proof is real-variable, not
Tauberian: integration by parts on
`integral (pi(t) - t/log t) t^{-(s-1)} dt / t^2` plus a
weighted-PNT-lite lemma `sum (log p) / p ~ log x`.

The cleanest rigidity statement in the literature about
`R(t) := N(t) - [t]`:

> **Conjecture 1.2 (Olofsson, open).** For every Beurling system
> different from the rational primes,
>
>     limsup_{x -> infinity} |N(x) - [x]| / log x  >  0.
>
> **Theorem 1.3.** For every `c > 0` there is a Beurling system
> other than the rational primes with `|N(x) - [x]| < c log x`
> for all `x >= 1`. (Construction: replace two primes `p_i, p_j`
> with the irrational `q = p_i p_j / (p_i + p_j - 1)`, giving
> `|N(x) - [x]| < (2/log q) log x + 6`.)
>
> **Proposition 3.1.** `|N(t) - [t]| = o(1)` iff the system is the
> rational primes.

So `log x` is the right threshold; the conjecture says it cannot
be beaten without recovering the rational primes themselves.

The conjecture's barrier is a `Re s = 0` analog of Lemma 7.2.4:

> **Proposition 3.3 (Olofsson).** No Beurling prime system has
> `zeta_P(s)` with a pole of order `n` at `s = 0` *and*
> `|N(x) - [x]| = o((log x)^n)`. The two are incompatible.

Proof: from `zeta_P(s) - zeta(s) = s integral_1^infinity R(t)/t^{s+1} dt`,
the assumed `o((log t)^n)` bound on `R` makes the integral
`o(s^{-n})` as `s -> 0+`, ruling out a pole of order `n`
(`zeta(s)` has no pole at `s = 0`). The conjecture would follow
from an extension of this no-poles property to all of `Re s = 0`,
currently open.

The structural lesson — **boundary behaviour of `zeta_P` on a
critical line constrains the size of an arithmetic remainder** —
is the same template as Debruyne's Lemma 7.2.4 (PNT vs Mertens at
`Re s = 1`), transported to `Re s = 0`. For BIDDER: any
next-order claim about an `M_n`-analog of `N - [.]` should look
for its barrier in the boundary behaviour of `zeta_{M_n}` on
`Re s = 0`.

## What this changes for BIDDER

### Sourced

S1. **The abstract Tauberian theorems are state-machine-portable.**
    Debruyne Theorem 2.1.3 / Theorem 2.2.1 take a locally `L^1`
    function `tau` on `[0, infinity)` satisfying a one-sided
    Tauberian condition (slowly decreasing, boundedly decreasing,
    or `e^{beta x} tau(x)` non-negative non-decreasing) whose
    Laplace transform has prescribed local pseudofunction (resp.
    pseudomeasure) boundary behaviour, and conclude an asymptotic
    for `tau`. **No UF, no Euler product** at the abstract level.
    **The dictionary into Beurling-prime arithmetic** (Lemma 7.2.4
    etc.) needs UF at two earlier points: reading `-zeta'_P/zeta_P`
    as the Mellin transform of a non-negative Chebyshev measure,
    and deriving its boundary behaviour from the Euler product.
    For non-UF `M_n` both steps fail; the failure of step (i) is
    not a gap in the proof technique but a hard consequence of
    `Q_n`'s signedness (see "Extended-sense" section). Using the
    abstract theorem on `M_n` requires constructing a *different*
    non-negative measure whose Mellin transform has the right
    boundary behaviour — one whose relation to `zeta_{M_n}` has
    to be supplied by hand.

S2. **Lemma 7.2.4 is the dictionary.** PNT-type asymptotics on
    `psi`-style sums ⇔ local pseudofunction boundary behaviour of
    a precisely-described combination of `zeta` and `1/(s-1)`. For
    any non-UF analog you build (e.g., `zeta_{M_n} - n^{-s}/(s-1)`?
    — to be derived), this is the form to aim for.

S3. **Diamond's theorem with explicit constants (Broucke
    Theorem 3.1.4).** `(P_alpha)` with constant `c` ⇒
    `N - rho x ≪ x exp(-(c(alpha+1))^{1/(alpha+1)} (log x . log_2 x)^{alpha/(alpha+1)} (1 + O(log_3 x / log_2 x)))`.
    The constant `(c(alpha+1))^{1/(alpha+1)}` is the sharp one
    (Theorem 3.1.5).

S4. **The extended-sense setup (Debruyne §1.2.1, Broucke §1.1)**
    is the framework one would *attempt* to fit `M_n` into. The
    convolution-exponential identity `dN = exp_* dPi` is the
    relation to verify or replace.

### Heuristic / extrapolation (BIDDER side)

H1. *Heuristic.* Broucke Theorem 2.1.2's "discretisation with
    `pi_P - F ≪ 1`" suggests a strategy: if BIDDER can construct a
    *measure-level* template `F` that approximates the
    prime-counting side of `M_n`'s structure (anchored by
    `zeta_{M_n}(s) = 1 + n^{-s} zeta(s)`), Broucke's procedure
    produces an actual discrete Beurling prime system whose
    `pi_P` tracks `F` to within `O(1)`. Note that this is an
    *approximation*, not a representation: per S1 above, `M_n`
    itself does not fit the extended-sense framework, so the
    discretisation can at best yield a Beurling system whose
    behaviour mimics `M_n`'s in some respects. Whether this
    gives BIDDER anything not already accessible analytically is
    not obvious.

H2. *Heuristic.* Olofsson's `log x` rigidity threshold for
    `|N - [.]|` (slide-deck reading) and the Malliavin
    `beta*(alpha) = alpha/(alpha+1)` law together suggest that
    `M_n`'s next-order corrections beyond the leading
    `(n - 1) k`-term observed in `experiments/acm-flow/mult-table/`
    might be governed by a similar transfer law from prime-side
    asymptotics to integer-side asymptotics. **This is an
    analogy, not a derivation.**

H3. *Heuristic.* The open problem of `[alpha, beta]`-system
    existence for `beta < 1/2, alpha > 1/2` parallels the
    BIDDER question of whether `Q_n` decompositions can have
    sharp prime-side cancellation but loose integer-side
    behaviour. Likely a different question, but the ceiling
    structure of Beurling primes (`max(alpha, beta) >= 1/2`)
    is the kind of constraint to look for.

The remaining chapters (Debruyne Ch. 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11;
Broucke Ch. 5, 6, 7, 8, 9) are deeper into specific Tauberian
refinements or unrelated analysis problems and are not on the
shortest path for BIDDER's questions.

### References outside the theses

For Beurling-style theory (UF-by-construction) and its abstract
generalisation:

- **Knopfmacher, *Abstract Analytic Number Theory* (1975).**
  Arithmetical semigroups: free commutative monoids on a
  countable set of "primes" with a norm function and an
  `O(x^delta)`-counting axiom. **UF holds by construction** —
  Knopfmacher's framework generalises Beurling–Landau but does
  not drop unique factorisation. The right line for number-field
  `O_K`-style analogs that retain UF; *not* the right line for
  `M_n`.

- **Diamond–Zhang, *Beurling Generalized Numbers* (AMS Math.
  Surveys & Monographs 213, 2016).** The standard textbook for
  Beurling theory itself. Cited repeatedly in the theses
  (Debruyne ref. [38], Broucke ref. [46]). Full treatment of the
  density theorems, sharp Mertens, Cesàro extensions, and the
  earlier-state-of-art on Malliavin's problems.

For the actually non-UF setting (`M_n`'s genuine framework):

- **Geroldinger–Halter-Koch, *Non-Unique Factorizations:
  Algebraic, Combinatorial and Analytic Theory* (Pure and
  Applied Mathematics, Chapman & Hall/CRC, 2006).** The canonical
  reference for non-UF factorisation theory: Krull monoids,
  C-monoids, BF-monoids, sets of lengths, elasticity invariants,
  and **arithmetic congruence monoids `M_n`** treated directly.
  The relevant invariants are combinatorial-monoid invariants of
  factorisation, not Beurling-style zeta-asymptotics. Beurling /
  Knopfmacher zeta machinery is in the rear-view mirror in this
  framework.

For the `Re s = 0` rigidity line (above):

- **Olofsson, *Properties of the Beurling generalized primes*
  (2010).** Mertens for Beurling primes plus the `log x`
  rigidity threshold for `|N - [.]|`.

- **Lagarias, *Beurling generalized integers with the Delone
  property*, Forum Math. 11 (1999) 295–312.** Classifies
  Beurling integer sequences with bounded gaps — the closest
  Beurling-side analog to BIDDER's "regular spacing"
  observations on the Champernowne side.
