# First calibration: kernel_zero

A worked example of the calibration discipline in `probes/README.md`,
recorded so future probe authors can see what the framework caught
and what it didn't. The probe is `probes/kernel_zero/`. The arc
below covers four calibration cycles before the probe passed all
three calibrations.

## What was probed

- **Target:** the algebra-defined zero set
  `{k ∈ [1, K] : Q_p(p^h k) = 0}` in a prime row of the Q-lattice,
  with `Q_p` exact-rational via `algebra/predict_q.q_general`.
- **Substrate:** one prime row of `q_lattice_4000_h{5,6,7,8}.npy`
  for `p ∈ {2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13}` and `K = 4000`.
- **Transducer:** identity (control), index reversal (known
  recovery), or synthetic uniform draws under identity (null).
- **Channels:** `no_op`, `zero_count`, `value_multiset`,
  `prime_row_identity_at_k1`. Two primary, one no-op control,
  one position-locked adversarial.
- **Anchor:** identity calibration passes 96/96 (cell, channel) at
  strength 1.0, TOL = 1e-12.

The full spec is `probes/kernel_zero/PROBE.md`.

## Calibration arc

Each cycle below is a calibration attempt followed by a diagnostic.
Per `probes/README.md`, calibration failures go to the human with
diagnostics, not to silent fix-and-retry.

### Cycle 1 — initial spec

- Predicted-zero set restricted to coprime cells:
  `{k ∈ [2, K] : gcd(k, p) = 1, Ω(k) ≤ h - 1}`, the literal
  `KERNEL-ZEROS.md` (i) classifier.
- Identity calibration: `no_op` and `prime_row_identity_at_k1`
  PRESENT at all 24 cells. `zero_count` ABSENT or PARTIAL across
  all 24 (lattice has more zeros than the coprime band predicts;
  strength climbs with `p` from 0.0 at `(2, 5)` to 0.916 at
  `(13, 8)`). `value_multiset` PARTIAL at three cells.
- Reverse calibration: `no_op` came back PARTIAL at strengths 0.85
  to 0.995 — the user's flagged "things trivial in ergodic-land
  are not so here" caveat materialized; the *predicted* "zeros
  moved away" effect didn't materialize because the row is
  zero-dominated.
- Null calibration: clean pass.

**Diagnosis (probe-spec bug).** The substrate is the *full* row,
which includes cells `k = p^t · k'` for `t ≥ 1`. Those cells
evaluate as `Q_p(p^{h+t} · k')` at a *higher* effective height by
the master expansion's overlap rule, and many of them are also
zero — the kernel-zero band at height `h + t` is wider. The
predicted-zero set should not have been restricted to coprime
cells when the substrate is the full row.

### Cycle 2 — sage / numpy plumbing

`AGENTS.md:22-23` documents that numpy / scipy / matplotlib are
available via `sage -python`. The cycle 1 probe was running pure
Python with a hand-rolled `.npy` reader. Switching to `sage
-python` and `numpy.load` produced numerically identical results,
confirming cycle 1's findings were arithmetic, not plumbing.

**Decision required.** Two ways to address the probe-spec bug:

- **(A)** Make the predicted-zero set algebra-defined:
  `{k : q_general(p, h, k) == Fraction(0)}`. Probe becomes a clean
  lattice-vs-algebra contract.
- **(B)** Restrict the substrate to coprime cells. Original spec
  matches by construction.

Picked (A): the substrate "row of the Q-lattice" is what's
actually cached and what downstream consumers see. (A) keeps the
probe as the lattice/algebra contract; (B) recasts as a different
probe.

### Cycle 3 — under (A)

- Identity calibration: 93/96 PRESENT. `zero_count` PRESENT at all
  24 cells (the (A) fix worked). Three `value_multiset` cells at
  `(p ∈ {3, 5, 7}, h = 8)` PARTIAL at strength 0.99975 (3999/4000
  entries match, one off).
- Reverse calibration: `no_op` PARTIAL across all 24 with strength
  0.82 to 0.997, exactly the reversal-symmetry quantity
  `|{k ∈ Z : K + 1 - k ∈ Z}| / |Z|` predicted in `PROBE.md`.
  Other channels match prediction.
- Null calibration: clean pass.

**Diagnosis (substrate-cache bug).** The three `value_multiset`
PARTIALs each had a single position where the lattice cell
differed from `float(q_general)` by `2.02e-12` — about 80,000 ULP
at magnitude 0.111, not single-ULP noise. The cells were
`(3, 8, k=1536), (5, 8, k=2560), (7, 8, k=3584)`, each `k = p ·
2^9`, the unique boundary cells `Ω(k') = h_eff = 9` in their
respective rows. By the kernel-zero boundary formula
(`KERNEL-ZEROS.md` (ii)) these cells are exactly `1/9`. The
cached float lattice was generated by computing the master
expansion in float, where the alternating sum reaches individual
terms `~10^5` and cancels to `10^-1` — float64 cannot hold that
contract; the residual `~10^-12` matched the cancellation floor.

**Decision required.** Three ways to address:

- **(α)** Accept as a finding; probe goes to menagerie.
- **(β)** Switch canonical substrate to `algebra` (compute via
  `q_general`); cached lattice becomes a secondary observation.
  Loses the lattice-vs-algebra primary contract.
- **(γ)** Regenerate the cached lattice in exact `Fraction`,
  convert to float at write time. Fixes the substrate where the
  bug actually is.

Picked (γ).

### Cycle 4 — under (γ)

`experiments/acm-champernowne/base10/q_distillery/q_lattice_4000_h_regen.py`
recomputes the four `q_lattice_4000_h{5,6,7,8}.npy` grids using
`q_general` (exact `Fraction`), converting to `float64` only at
the final assignment. Old buggy versions preserved as
`q_lattice_4000_h{...}.float_buggy.bak.npy`. Total runtime ~22
minutes (3999×4000 cells × 4 heights at h=8 worst-case).

After regen, max `|lattice - algebra|` is `0.000e+00` across all
24 cells in the probe panel (verified at TOL=1e-12 and tighter).

- Identity calibration: 96/96 PRESENT at strength 1.0.
- Reverse: as predicted in cycle 3.
- Null: clean.

`anchors.py` reports `ANCHOR PASS  all 96 (cell, channel)
verdicts PRESENT at strength 1.0`. `algebra/test_anchors.py`
A1..A10 unchanged after the lattice swap.

## What the framework caught

- **The cycle 1 probe-spec bug.** Without the `zero_count` channel
  failing systematically across the panel, the mismatch between
  "coprime kernel-zero band" and "full row of zeros" would have
  been invisible — the `no_op` channel happily reports PRESENT
  because every coprime kernel-zero position *is* zero in the
  lattice. The discipline of "include a count-of-zeros invariant
  alongside the position-locked control" is what surfaced the
  bug.
- **The cycle 3 substrate-cache bug.** The `value_multiset`
  PARTIAL at strength 0.99975 looked like float noise. Reading
  off the affected cells and computing the actual diff
  (`2.02e-12` ≈ 80,000 ULP, not single-ULP) showed it was
  structural cancellation in the lattice cache's generator. The
  TOL = 1e-12 setting (committed to in `PROBE.md` as machine
  tolerance) is what made the cancellation visible.
- **The reverse calibration's "ergodic-land" effect.** The user
  flagged this in advance: "things trivial in ergodic-land are
  not so here, perhaps." Cycle 1 showed `no_op` not destroyed by
  reversal as naively predicted, because the row is zero-dominant
  at small primes and reversal preserves the zero set's
  structure. Cycle 3 made this quantitative: `no_op` strength
  under reversal *equals* the algebra-zero set's reversal
  symmetry, `|{k ∈ Z : K + 1 - k ∈ Z}| / |Z|`. The PROBE.md
  prediction was rewritten to match, replacing "ABSENT" with
  "PARTIAL".

## What the framework didn't catch

- **The lattice-vs-algebra contract was unstated before the
  probe.** Nothing in `algebra/` documented that the cached
  `.npy` was supposed to match `q_general` at machine tolerance.
  The probe made the contract explicit; the contract failed at
  h=8 boundary cells; the regen fixed it. A pre-existing
  invariant in `algebra/test_anchors.py` cross-checking the
  lattice would have caught this earlier.
- **Numpy's availability via sage.** The hand-rolled `.npy`
  reader in cycle 1 was technical debt the framework didn't
  predict. The fix was a one-line invocation change. No
  framework rule prevented or surfaced this; the user pointed at
  `AGENTS.md:22-23`.

## Findings carried forward

- The cached `q_lattice_4000_h{5,6,7,8}.npy` files match
  `algebra/predict_q.q_general` at exact zero (TOL=1e-12 trivially
  satisfied). This is now an unstated lattice-vs-algebra contract
  in the repo; future probes can cite it.
- The reverse-symmetry of the algebra-zero set,
  `|{k ∈ Z : K + 1 - k ∈ Z}| / |Z|` for `Z = {k : Q_p(p^h k) = 0,
  k ∈ [1, K]}`, is computable per (p, h) and matches the probe's
  `no_op`-under-reverse strength at all 24 panel cells.
- The lattice generator at high `h` and boundary cells
  (`Ω(k') = h_eff`) requires exact-rational arithmetic; float
  arithmetic suffers cancellation loss `~10^-12` at h=8 boundary
  cells. The regen script
  `q_distillery/q_lattice_4000_h_regen.py` is parameterised on
  `OUT_DTYPE` for future regularization probes that need
  bf16/half/etc.

## Discipline notes for future probes

- **Run all three calibrations on the first attempt.** Even if
  identity is failing, run reverse and null too; the cross-pattern
  is what diagnoses (e.g., `no_op` failing under both identity and
  reverse with high strength meant the substrate had pre-existing
  symmetry, not that the transducer was weak).
- **Don't silently fix calibration failures.** Cycles 1 and 3 each
  ended with a brief to the human and an A/B/(γ) decision. The
  framework explicitly forbids the agent from picking which kind
  of fix to apply when the choice changes what the probe is
  testing.
- **The `value_multiset` channel did its job.** It was the only
  channel that surfaced the substrate-cache bug; the `no_op` and
  `zero_count` channels were satisfied because the buggy cells
  were near non-zero exact values. Permutation-invariant channels
  on the multiset of cell values are a high-leverage way to catch
  numerical residuals that position-locked channels miss.
- **Boundary cells are where high-h substrates fail.** At
  `Ω(k') = h_eff`, the master expansion's alternating sum has
  individual terms of magnitude `~h^h / ∏ e_i!` cancelling to
  `O(1/h)`. Float precision degrades by the cancellation depth.
  A probe at TOL = machine ε only works on substrates generated
  in exact arithmetic.
