# Gap Weather

The same ACM survivor window is passed through several ambient
numerical semigroups `S = ⟨a,b⟩`. For each `S`, keep only the shared
survivors `C_SurvA`: integers that survive the ACM bundle and are also
multiplicative atoms of `(S, ·)`.

Each horizontal horizon is the signed discrepancy residual

    F_L(t) - t

of the shifted decimal-tail orbit of that `C_SurvA` digit stream. Warm
area means the orbit has accumulated too much mass below `t`; cool area
means too little. The height of the weather is the shape behind the
single number `D_L*`.

The gaps of `S` are not drawn directly. They are the pressure system
that bends the survivor stream.
